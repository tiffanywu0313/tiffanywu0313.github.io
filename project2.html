Yelp Redesign

How might we redesign yelp for university students planning a group outing where different people have constraints or planning a date to multiple locations?



Product:       Website redesign

Team:             Tiffany Wu, Wesley Yuen, Shirley Zeng,
                          Megha Singhania, Ruperto Torres

Role:               UX Research, UI Design, Report Writing

Tools:             Figma, Miro

Timeline:     September - November 2019

Background

This was a course project that I worked on in a team of 5 students. We wanted to understand how people are using crowdsourced business recommendations on Yelp.



Pilot + Initial User Research

To learn more about the problem space, we wanted to conduct a user evaluation to learn how users are utilizing the platform and what the main pain points were. In our evaluation, we had a series of tasks that we would ask the user to complete while we observed, then, we would follow up with a short semi-structured interview asking the experience. After our pilot runs, we decided to print out the tasks list we asked each user to complete as users had a hard time remembering the details.

After analyzing the data we collected in the form of an Affinity Diagram, we learned that most users only use Yelp to look up recommendations of food when planning outings, and that the Yelp navigation feature (map too small or doesn’t show directions under 4 blocks) and inconsistent filters were the major pain points.

Thus, we decided to further investigate how we can redesign Yelp so that it better supports planning a group outing with two or more people.



Cognitive walkthrough on low-fi

Based on feedback from the user evaluation, we split up into 2 small teams to brainstorm possible designs and came up with two conceptual designs. I was part of the team that came up with a map based design (similar to that of google maps). The other team’s design focused on tweaking components of the original design.

Map based prototype

List based prototype

We performed a cognitive walkthrough of both lo-fi prototypes and decided to merge good ideas from both prototypes into one. Our mid-fi prototype was thus created after refining our lo-fi prototypes.



Usability testing on mid-fi

Our Mid fi prototype was an interface that allowed users to create events where they can add other users, these events would become filters that the user can use to search for restaurants etc. This was also layered on top of a map based interface where the location/distance to and from the restaurant was easy to see.

To validate our solution, we conducted a user study with our mid-fi prototype. We made the user do multiple tasks on the prototype while talking aloud their thinking process and collected data from a follow up interview. Our results were quite positive as most users thought the interface was very straightforward to use.



Reflection

Through this process, I’ve learned the importance of iteration on products as even when you think you’ve got all the problems down, users or your teammates will give feedback that will surprise you and is something you’ve never thought about. The challenging part about solving this problem was the lack of time we had and the similarity of the Yelp interface to other food/business recommendation related services. If we had more time, we would definitely have tested our prototypes on more people and had a better grasp of all possible pain points. If I were to do this again, I think I would try to have my teammates practice conducting usability testing more so our tests sessions were more consistent, as some teammates are better at probing during interviews than others etc.
