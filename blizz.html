A responsive web experience designed for a plant-based food subscription service.

In the fast paced cities of Taiwan, many workers have a hard time eating healthy and setting aside time to cook. To save these people the time and effort of worrying about their diets, we set out to create a service that would cater to their needs. Thus, Blizz was created as a plant-based food subscription service that targets modern day people who are too busy to prepare, cook or eat healthy.

Tools Used: Figma, Adobe Illustrator, Powerpoint, Pen and Paper.

Responsibilities: I worked on this project during my internship at Huemon Design Agency this past summer. I worked in a team of 5 people including me (UX/UI designer), a graphic designer, a product manager, an engineer, and a marketing manager. My main responsibilities included wireframing, prototyping, copywriting, and researching CSR programs.



Background

Blizz was proposed by a startup client to the design agency I interned at, and were in the early stages when I joined to work on the web interface. I often participated in their discussions of brand positioning and core values.

Brand Character

Blizz wanted to have the characteristics of Educator, Leader, and Protector.

Brand Mission

1. Effective - providing nutrients and health benefits for the body while decreasing metabolism burden.
2. Convenient - saving people time by providing food and nutritional knowledge.
3. Sustainable - using zero waste or sustainable packaging, as well as supporting local farmers.
4. Delicious - challenging the boundaries of good flavor by not adding additives and directly freezing fresh local produce.

Reflection

What I learned

1. Visual Design Process
Having come from a computer science background, I don’t have a lot of experience with visual design. While working closely with the graphic designer in my team, I got to learn more about their creative process and ways to seamlessly incorporate branding into a product.

2. Working in a interdisciplinary team
During my internship, I got to work in an interdisciplinary team with engineers, product managers, and other designers. This made me realize the importance of communication and explaining design decisions to non-designers in a clear and non-jargon way. As well, I learned to create design systems for better hand off to developers.

Limitations and Future Direction

As time was limited, we were only able to do cognitive walkthroughs with a couple users on the early prototypes. In the future, more user testing on the prototype would be beneficial to validating the user flow and discovering any pain points that the design may have.

My design was signed off for development at the end of my internship, so I look forward to seeing it be developed and iterated on as more testing happens in the development stages.
