Takeaways

1. Working with Content
During my time at Sovos, I got to work with a dedicated content team and it was very insightful. I learned a lot about the importance of content. Good content takes the guessing away from the user and replaces it with “knowing”. Users shouldn’t ever have to "guess" what a button does, rather, they should “know” what it does.

2. Working with Design Systems
I also got to help create components and maintain documentation for the Sovos Mosaic design system. It’s very interesting to see how each atom is the building block of a whole page or product and there are a lot of things to consider when crafting design system elements such as accessibility.

3. Getting feedback on B2B products
Since the products/projects that I worked on at Sovos were mostly B2B, I had to be creative with finding ways to get feedback on my designs. Interviewing customers/end users was not an option due to product nature and time constraints, thus finding internal I reached out to Sovos users (service team and QA team) instead. However, since these users use the interface regularly and are expert users, when asked what could be improved with the interface they are often at a standstill and need more time to think. So I realized that it was most efficient and helpful to start with asking how they use the product and ask them to quickly demonstrate how they usually complete the task on the interface.

4. Domain knowledge and getting context
One of the things that I learned during my time at Sovos was picking up on domain knowledge quickly and getting enough context for my projects. I realized that I don’t need to know everything about a product to get started, but rather, I need to figure out what is within the scope of my project and ask questions to get enough context to unblock myself.
