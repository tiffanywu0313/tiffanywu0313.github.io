Tools Used: Figma, Figjam, Dovetail, SurveyMonkey, Pendo, Teams, Excel.

Duration: June - August 2023 (3 months).

Responsibilities: I was the UX Design Intern for Sovos’ North America Design Team for Summer 2023. During this role I took on many projects including conducting exploratory research on how custom tax rules are set and edited, redesigning pages and processes for tax software, and assisting with updating and documentation of Sovos’ Mosaic Design System.





What is Sovos?

Sovos is a company that specializes in tax compliance solutions. They provide a range of software and services to help businesses and organizations meet their tax compliance and reporting needs.









Takeaways

1. Working with Content
This was the first time that I got to work with a dedicated content team. I really learned a lot from our content designer about the importance of content. Good content takes the guessing away from the user and instead replaces it with “knowing”. Users shouldn’t ever have to guess what a button does, rather, they should “know” what it does.

2. Working with Design Systems
This was also the first time that I got to help maintain and use a comprehensive design system. It’s very interesting to see how each atom is the building block of a whole page or product and there are a lot of things to consider when crafting design system elements such as accessibility.

3. B2B product pain points
I’ve noticed that interviewing B2B product users is different from interviewing customers/end users. Sovos users are usually experts since they use the interface regularly. Thus, when asked what could be better about the interface they are often at a standstill and need more time to think.

4. Domain knowledge and getting context
One of the main things I learned during my internship was picking up on domain knowledge quickly and getting enough context for my projects. I don’t need to know everything about a product to work on individual projects but rather I need to figure out what is within the scope of my project and ask questions so I get enough context that I can unblock myself and get started.
